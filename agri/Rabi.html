Rabi Crop Types                                                             Rabi crops or rabi harvest are rural crops that are planted in winter and harvested in the spring in India. The term is derived from the Arabic word for "spring", which is used in the Indian subcontinent, where it is the spring harvest (otherwise called the "winter crop").
In Indian farming, The rabi crops include wheat, barley, oats (cereals), chickpea/gram (pulses), linseed, garlic, cumin, coriander, mustard (oilseeds) etc
